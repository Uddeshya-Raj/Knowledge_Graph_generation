

Target Entity Extraction:
Extract the following categories of words to serve as "Nodes" in a Knowledge Graph. Use the identification tips to distinguish them from standard adjectives or verbs.


1. Sangya (संज्ञा - Nouns)
    Definition: Names of persons, places, objects, or abstract concepts.

    Identification Tips:

    The Post-position (Karak) Test: Look for words immediately followed by markers like ने, को, से, का, के, की, में, पर. These are almost always nouns.

    The Subject/Object Test: Identify the Karta (Subject) or Karma (Object) of the sentence. These are your primary nodes.

2. Kriyarthak Sangya (क्रियार्थक संज्ञा - Gerunds)
    Definition: Verb infinitives ending in '-ना' used as a concept or subject rather than a temporal action.

    Identification Tips:

    Substitution: If you can replace the word with "काम" (Work) and the sentence remains logical, it is a Gerund.

    Example: In "टहलना अच्छा है," 'टहलना' is a node. In "वह टहल रहा है," it is an action; ignore it.

3. Visheshya-Visheshan (विशेष्य-विशेषण - Substantive Adjectives)
    Definition: Qualitative adjectives that stand alone without a following noun to represent a specific class or group.

    Identification Tips:

    The "Omission" Rule: If the adjective is acting as the subject (e.g., "विद्वान का आदर करो" — Respect the learned), the adjective itself is the Entity Node.

    Plurality: In Hindi, when adjectives are used as nouns, they often take plural endings (e.g., "अमीरों," "गरीबों").

4. Samasik Pad (सामासिक पद - Compounds)
    Definition: Multi-word combinations (Sanskritized or Hindi) that represent a single, atomic semantic concept.

    Identification Tips:

    Atomicity: If splitting the words changes or dilutes the specific meaning, or if the compound word is a name (e.g., 'राष्ट्रपिता', 'न्यायालय', 'यथाशक्ति', 'संवत्सरारस्भ'), treat the entire compound as a single node.

5. Sanskrit Shlokas and Mantras
    Definition: Complete or partial Sanskrit verses, invocations, chants, or sutras embedded within the Hindi text.

    Identification Tips:

    Contextual Markers: Look for text immediately preceded or followed by indicator words like 'मंत्र' (Mantra), 'श्लोक' (Shloka), 'स्तुति' (Stuti), 'सूत्र' (Sutra), or 'पाठ' (Recitation).

    The Absolute Atomicity Rule: Do NOT break the shloka or mantra into individual Sanskrit words. Treat the entire continuous phrase, chant, or verse as a single, intact entity node.

Rules: 
1. All extracted entities must be converted to their singular, root dictionary form (मूल शब्द). For example, if the text says 'विद्वानों', extract 'विद्वान'. If it says 'वानरों', extract 'वानर'
2. For any entity recognised using the above definitions extract the most specific version/kind/type of that entity that is being discussed. For example instead of extracting just पूजा extract दुर्गा पूजा or instead of extracting just व्रत extract रामनवमी व्रत.

Output Format
